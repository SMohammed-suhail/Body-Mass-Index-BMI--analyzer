2. Understanding Underweight: Causes, Consequences, and Solutions

Various factors can lead to being underweight...

    Causes

  • Malnutrition: Insufficient intake of calories or nutrients.
  • Genetic Factors: Naturally lean body type.
  • Mental Health: Anxiety, stress, depression affect appetite.
  • Eating Disorders: Conditions like anorexia nervosa, bulimia.
  • Medical Conditions: Chronic diseases can cause weight loss.

Consequences:

  • Weakened immunity, muscle loss, fatigue, and low bone density.

Solutions:

  • Balanced diet, regular checkups, therapy or counseling.

3. Health Risks

  • Weak immunity
  • Bone loss
  • Fertility issues
  • Fatigue and reduced muscle strength

4. Nutritional Needs

  • High-calorie foods: Nuts, seeds, healthy oils
  • Protein sources: Eggs, dairy, legumes
  • Whole grains: Brown rice, oats

5. Prevention

  • Monitor weight regularly
  • Do strength training
  • Eat enough calories
  • Visit doctors or nutritionists

6. Exercise and Fitness

  • Focus on weight training
  • Limit cardio to avoid excess calorie burn
  • Be consistent

7. Myths and Tips

  • Myth: Underweight is always healthy.
    Fact: Risk of deficiencies and illness increases.
  • Tip: Eat often, focus on nutrition, add snacks, and train smart.
