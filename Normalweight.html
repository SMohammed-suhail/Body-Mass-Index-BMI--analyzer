Understanding Normal Weight

Introduction

Normal weight is defined as a body mass index (BMI) between 18.5 and 24.9. Maintaining a normal weight is essential for good health, reducing the risk of various chronic diseases and promoting longevity and energy.

Health Implications

Normal BMI range is associated with reduced risk of diabetes, cardiovascular disease, hypertension, and more.

Weight Management

Eating healthy, staying active, managing stress, and sleeping well are keys to maintaining normal weight.

Why Maintaining Normal Weight is Important

  • Better mental health
  • Lower disease risk
  • Improved physical mobility
  • Higher life expectancy

Tips for a Healthy Lifestyle

  • Eat a variety of healthy foods
  • Control portions
  • Exercise regularly
  • Stay hydrated
  • Track your weight regularly

Normal Weight: Myths and Facts

  • Myth: Skipping meals helps maintain weight
    Fact: It can slow metabolism and lead to overeating later
  • Myth: Healthy weight means zero fat
    Fact: Body needs essential fat for hormones and function

Fitness

Stay active with both aerobic (e.g., walking, jogging) and strength exercises for muscle tone and heart health.
